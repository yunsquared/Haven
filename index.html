Our Sub-Organizations

Haven proudly serves as an administrative umbrella for most of the student organizations involved in the support and outreach to LGBTQ+ students at Cornell. Haven provides a vehicle for these groups to secure funding and on-going training, and consultation with professional staff.

Source: Haven

ACE

The Ace/Asexual Support Group at Cornell

ACE is open to all people who identify, or feel that they might identify within the ace umbrella. The ace umbrella includes asexual people and those who occupy the gray space between sexuality and asexuality. Please note that ace people might experience other forms of attraction, such as romantic, aesthetic, emotional, or intellectual.

Contact

CBA

Cornell Business Alliance

The Cornell Business Alliance was established in 2010, as a professional undergraduate organization providing support for LGBTQ+ students with an interest in business. CBA works with prominent firms to further enhance their diversity recruitment efforts by connecting them with our talented student members. They continue to successfully accomplish their mission by informing LGBT undergraduates of career opportunities, attending annual conferences, facilitating networking opportunities with corporate recruiters to provide familiarity of their firm’s efforts in diversity and inclusion, and helping members establish mentorships with supportive alumni.

Contact

CRUNCH

For Kink/Queer Community

Crunch is a peer run group committed to education, social support, and programming centering on kink and its place in the queer community. Kink is how many people engage with themselves and others, either sexually or non-sexually, in ways that challenge mainstream norms that prescribe gender, sexuality, relationships, power, intimacy, kinship, and uses of the body. Crunch explores the ideas around and practices of kink in queer spaces.

Contact

Ga’avah​

A Space for LGBTQ+ Jewish Students

A group for students that are Jewish or have a relationship with Judaism and also are LGBTQ+. This group will provide a community space and have regular meetings and attend Jewish events as a group. The group continuously explore the intersections between Jewish identity and LGBTQ+ identity.

Contact

Lavender​

For lesbian, bisexual, transgender, queer, and questioning women and nonbinary people

A confidential peer run social, support, and event planning organiztion for lesbian, bisexual, queer, questioning, and trans women on campus as well as nonbinary people. This group creates community, discusses issues that specifically affect LGBT women and nonbinary people, and ensures that LGBT women and nonbinary people have a place to be their authentic selves.

Contact

MOSAIC​

For Queer and Trans People of Color

Mosaic is a peer social support organization for LGBTQ people of color. Mosaic’s social goals include giving QTPOC a place to belong, weekly meetings, supporting QTPOC speakers to come to campus, and organizing activities and events geared towards awareness of their specific issues.

Contact

Oasis​

For Asian, Pacific Islander, and Desi/American LGBTQ+ Students

A group for Asian, Pacific Islander, and Desi/ American LGBTQ+ students. This group provides community and support for APIDA LGBTQ+ students. They have weekly meetings and continue to collaborate with other APIDA student groups and organizations.

Contact

OUTreach​

For gay, bisexual, transgender queer, and questioning Men and nonbinary people

OUTreach is a group for anyone in the Cornell community interested in coming together arouund issues of being a man and gay, bisexual, queer, and/or transgender as well as nonbinary. One objective is to reach out to other members of the LGBTQ community and the broader Cornell and Ithaca communities. Another objective is to support and educate on navigating male spaces as an LGBTQ+ individual.

QPA​

LGBTQ+ Pre-Health Association

The Queer Pre-Health Alliance is looking for LGBTQ+ students who are interested in the pre-health field (such as pre-med or any health related carrer). This group serves to prepare students for the medical field, specifically considering their LGBTQ identities.

Contact

Reading Rainbow​​

Queer Literature Club

Reading Rainbow is a club that meets to discuss articles, short stories, and books related to LGBTQ+ issues. We will use the readings to have more in-depth discussions of various issues and hear the perspectives of club members and those who are not club members. Queer students and allies are encouraged to join!

Transgender, Agender, Non-Binary, Genderqueer, Other

TANGO is a group for all Cornellians who identify as transgender and/or non-binary, or are questioning their gender identities. Providing a space for community, support, and community advice, trans and non-binary students.
